We reached the anomaly in the morning, and it was indeed a massive rock in the ground.

Not sure what we can do about that.

Not sure what it THOUGHT we could do about that.

None of us wanted to go near it, but we thought we'd either need to pass by that meteor or the Lesser Wastes to continue south. We weren't sure what would be the lesser of two evils, so we decided to make a quick detour to the settlement of Eltreum nearby.

This turned out to be a very bad decision.

The city itself wasn't too strange. The entire place has been overrun with Scarlet Whisper, and all the inhabitants are largely laid out by the drug. No one paid us too much mind at first, and we were able to pass through the city without much fanfare. Even Noggin came with us into the city, and we had no one jumping at our throats.

We left it to Grem to find us information from the locals about the path forward. She seemed to put on some sort of comedy act for the citizens, which they found very amusing. Unfortunately, this wasn't really getting us information, so Luisa ended up asking around and getting us details instead.

Meanwhile, Noggin had disappeared.

Luisa and the rest of us were able to talk to the locals and get a little more information about paths to the capital of the region. We learned that there's pretty much no path forward that isn't perilous. It we head towards the meteorite we saw earlier, it'll drive us insane. If we head towards the black lake south of here, boats don't make it across. We were in the midst of discussing which option might be better when things started to take a turn for the worse.

A strange feminine figure approached Grem, far better dressed than most of the other residents of the city. They carried a parasol with them, and had eerily pale skin. I suspected they might be a vampire, and Iris confirmed that vampires were a real creature in her original world. We attempted to view the vampire through a mirror, and succeeded. We asked the residents about the newcomer, and they very quietly insisted that they did not know the individual and that we should stop talking immediately .

The vampire(?) and Grem talked amicably for a while, and appeared to be leaving together. The rest of us got up to join them, but they vanished together into thin air. We called out to Grem, but at the same time a large fireball went off in an adjacent building.

We rushed to the scene to find Noggin near death, and another vampire raging against him, calling him a vermin and attempting to finish him off. We intervened and attempted to dissuade him from attacking Noggin. The vampire, however, ripped himself in half (along the torso) and attacked two of our party members at the same time. Rather notably, it appeared to bite and drain the blood from Noggin, growing stronger in the process.

Our attacks were not entirely ineffective, but neither did it seem likely that we'd be able to kill this vampire. He was fast, strong, and seemed consumed by bloodlust. We planned instead to try to extract Noggin, and slowly escape however we could.

Thankfully, this proved unnecessary, since the vampire was not immune to clerical magic. I was able to use Tazaam's sigil to ward him off.

I've typically used a hasty crucifix for Turn Undead spells in the past, but it seemed like the better idea in the moment. In truth, I try not to use this spell often because I find it finicky at best. Unfortunately, it is a standard of clerics to know the spell, and I was not allowed to practice medicine without knowledge of the ability back when I first started travelling.

My instructor, Cleric Tonoway with the orkish tribes, insisted it was the only spell a cleric needed to learn, and that it'd work on any manner of undead creature. He initially demonstrated the effect with a well-preserved pickled eyeball he carried on his person at all times. Tonoway insisted that merely the reminder that his god Gruumsh watched over the lands would drive undead into a frenzied panic, and waved that eyeball around at anything remotely threatening while we were out travelling. I swear he once waved it at a stray rabbit, and performed a full prayer of gratitude once the poor thing took off, scared out of its mind by the approaching orc.

Regardless to say I don't use the spell often, nor do I use an eyeball. The crucifixes seem to work against some lesser undead, but I haven't gotten much use with them for bigger issues. I don't think I've been in a situation though where a knife or some loud shouting has worked any less than a show of divinity. Not to mention, not all the undead I've come across have been hostile.

But it works against vampires! I'm happy to have figured that much out. I don't worship Tazaam's but I do keep the sigil on me at all times anyways. It seems to help me out a bit, carrying the red light I've got currently, and if it repels vampires then I'll be sure to make good use of that while I can.

In any case, divine power seems to repel the vampires. The one that was attacking Noggin insisted it'd remember its faces, before it ran off. We ran out towards the lake to regroup with Noggin, but haven't seen any sign of Grem. Whatever happened to her, Noggin doesn't seem to know about it.

Noggin, apparently, was off on his own investigating matters. He'd found a room filled with reports, and a pair of legs missing their upper-body. The owner of said legs was the vampire we'd encountered, who sniffed him out and cornered him before we had arrived.

He got a good bite in on Noggin, far deeper than I would have expected the fangs to go. The wound has a slight corkscrew, and went pretty deep into the neck. It looked clean, and I'm not too concerned about any symptoms from it (magical or otherwise), but Noggin is definately missing a lot of blood.

We put some distance between ourselves and the city, but it doesn't feel very safe out here. We're staying in Noggin's tiny hut tonight to try to reduce our presence. Luisa was helping me train far into the night, and has only now retired so she can get a little rest in before morning.

I don't intend to rely on divine power if a vampire shows up between now and when we start in the morning. But, I may burn an offering to the ever-watching Gruumsh whenever we get a chance to light a fire. I don't sleep, so maybe my gifts really are from Tonoway's gods. I think I'd like that better than Tazaam, at any rate.
