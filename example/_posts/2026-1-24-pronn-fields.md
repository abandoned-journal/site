The envoy from Riawat dropped us off on the northern edge of the Pronn Fields. Our journey back over the Punished Sea wasn't bad at all, nothing of import happened there.

We landed along the coastline, with a large fungal forest stretching out as far as the eye could see. Glowing mushrooms made up the entirety of the field, tall enough to blot out the sun as we passed underneath.

We investigated the mushrooms a little, but found them mostly harmless. The ground beneath them was stranger, changing and moving slightly on its own. Again, though, we found it mostly harmless. The only trouble we had with the ground was that it did not show our footprints well, save for Luisa's which were burnt into the surface.

Navigating the forest was a challenge. While we weren't getting lost, progress was slow due to just how similar every place looked. We continued forwards though, hoping things wouldn't get worse.

The only out-of-place thing we found in the forest were some small hexagonal stones. They formed a perimeter for as far as we could see. We couldn't identify the stones, but didn't see any immediate danger either. After passing their threshold, all the magicians in the party were able to hear a sort of radio-static in the air. Again, strange, but harmless.

As we travelled, we attempted to mess with the radio frequency. It responded to Grem's music, and later to telepathy. We were able to make contact with the Queen of the mushroom fields, some sort of entity that made up the entirety of the forest.

At first there was miscommunication between us. The Queen attempted to remove us from the forest, being unable to understand what we were. It summoned several small constructs made of bone and mycelium, which on their own were not much trouble for our group. I made the mistake of trying to remove the constructs with magic, however, and that seemed to empower them. Thankfully, the Queen had little interest in actually fighting us, and we were able to once again establish peace between our parties.

The Queen implored our help in removing two anomalies that were preventing her max range from growing, one to the south, and another to the south east. We surmised these were likely the Damned Sun, and Lesser Decay marked on our maps, though we were not familiar with either.

She was desperate for our help, but we were unsure of how we'd deal with either anomaly, and uncertain of whether the mushrooms spreading would be a good thing in the long run. We attempted to placate her by asking for other ways of helping her, but she didn't seem to want much else we could provide. If we pass through again, however, we should attempt to bring her microcontrollers. She seemed to accept gifts of raw metal, but seems to want something more processed. 

As we neared the border of her range, the Queen once again begged for our help. We told her we were heading in the direction of the anomaly, and would do what we can. She was overjoyed and directed us to the exit of the forest.

The journey took us the entire day, and while we haven't reached the anomaly yet, we're stopping to rest. We're outside of the Queen's range, but still beneath the mushroom trees, so it seems like a relatively safe place to sleep.

Luisa told us to "do burpees until failure" tonight, so I'll be continuing those until the others awake. Mind you, I can't do more than a few at a time. I'll have to take some breaks, but I bet I can make it until morning. I haven't passed out yet.
