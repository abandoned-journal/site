We were found in the morning by the denizens of the island. A scouting party found our ship and approached peacefully, so we did the same.

They welcomed us to their city. Apparently it's a common occurrence for ships to wash up on their shore, sent from Dagatar's people as a death sentence. It seems this has backfired, as they've built a thriving community free from the rule of the four gods here.

However, they do hold the lightless god, Nullity, in reverence. We found this out immediately, since Noggin was carrying her around as a parasite.

The lightless appear to be able to see her, but the rest of us do not. I don't doubt their testimony though, given the sheer number of citizens who were agape at Noggin as we walked through their city. We now know how Noggin was able to defeat Dagatar.

The scouts of Riawat were quite welcoming, and gave us lodging while they spoke with their elders about our arrival. We weren't particularily appreciative or patient about that matter. We planned exit routes, tested out Noggin's ability to hide if need be, and even had Grem spy on the scouts to make sure no surprises were heading our way. 

Thankfully we had little to worry about. The city elders were eager to meet us, and held no ill intent towards Noggin.

We spoke with them awhile and learned a bit more about the situation this world had been cast in. Dagatar and the other three gods were indeed bad news.

Prior to them, this land had been ruled mainly by Oni. They were displaced by the parasitic gods that gave us all the lights we now bear. These gods seem to be the ones bringing people to this land from far away, claiming new citizens for their own factions so they can slowly suck dry their very life essence.

I was surprised to learn that Dagatar's death is the first of its kind. No gods have been slain before, we had truly done something [parts here have been erased] new. Riawat celebrated the death of Dagatar, and insisted we continue on to slay the other gods of this land.

We were understandably uncomfortable with this request. At first, there was a great question regarding Noggin's goddess Nullity and her connection to our group. The elders insisted that Nullity was a boon to the people, one of the parasitic gods who had gone against her nature and been sealed away. However, we have never spoken with Nullity, and were unaware of her presence until now. She seems either unwilling or unable to communicate with us, even with Noggin as her host. Seeing as we need to rely on her god-killing powers, I feel there's some dissent about whether this goddess is going to be a reliable partner.

Our deeper concern, however, was regarding the citizen's of each god. Dagatar drained his people and all but killed them when he was dying. We adamantly refused to do the same to the rest of the world's population.

This was a contentious point between us and the elders, who argued that the fate of every light-bearer would be a slow death anyhow. We argued against this plan vehemently, and eventually they relented agreeing that a better solution would have to be found.

It was the condition of Dedrik (and Grem, though they did not single her out) that convinced us a  better route was possible. Both had their lights removed without any ill effects, and ideally that could be done for others as well. Unfortunately, it seemed like it'd be a fool's errand to try to get everyone to go through the ritual before the gods were killed.

However, the elders mentioned there was an artifact we could seek out: a needle rapier. We suspect its current owner is Halfdane, known to these people as the trickster god Kytheo. To these people, he and his sword are myth, but we're fairly certain of his existence. Should we be able to find him or his blade in the south, we can hopefully sever the lights of a people before killing their god. That is the only way forwards, far as we are concerned.

This does, however, mean that we now need to travel far south and then… attempt to kill three gods. Hopefully Nullity will be doing the heavy lifting, but I have a suspicion that we were only able to kill Dagatar because he underestimated us. The other gods are aware of his death, and likely aware that we may turn on them as well. We can burn that bridge when we get to it though.

Our true purpose in coming to Riawat was to scout ahead and eventually find passageway for the refugees in the south to make their way here. Riawat was… welcoming, but unwilling or unable to do much to finish our quest. They welcomed any people that could reach their shores, but couldn't provide much more than that.

Dedrik bullied them into sending parties to the south to go retrieve the lightless. It took some convincing, but with Dagatar's people out of the way, the northern land should be relatively safe to traverse. It seems they'll be handling things from here.

Last Gasp also appears to be connected to these people, likely an outpost that'll grow now that the land is up for grabs. Hopefully they don't mind too much that we stole a ship from them. I suppose we returned it, in a way?

It was late into the morning when we finished talking with the elders. Both of our parties were satisfied with the arrangements that had been made, but it had taken quite a few hours to hammer out details and come up with a plan that isn't either suicidal or negligent of some group of people or another. They speak of great prophecies and plans within plans for their people and gods, and we're more concerned with the logistics and ethics of matters. Playing between those lines took some time.

Now that things are over though, they've returned us to our quarters. We'll be discussing supplies and suppliers starting tomorrow morn, but for now everyone has taken to bed. I'm going to head out for a drink.
