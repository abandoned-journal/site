Shortly after heading out of the Lessar Decay, we ran into trouble.

We were trying to avoid Polovora, which was full of circling vampires when we saw it off in the distance. We decided to skirt along the land in the general direction of Plantation Valley, where we found a smuggler's route marked in theives' cant. We were already a bit suspicious moving into the route, since we saw that the markings had been modified at some point, but the path looked clear ahead and we were eager to get away from the capital.

We travelled alone for some time, but a caravan found its way behind us. We flagged them down and tried to cooperate with them to find a place to let them pass, but they insisted it was no bother and suggested we camp together for the night. They shared with us some food and drink, and tried to pry a bit into our reasons for being out here. We truthfully confessed we were just passing through.

Noggin must have caught wind of something, he was the first to strike. Noggin struck down a number of men, and all of us reacted quickly after that, taking up arms against the smugglers. Another caravan was coming down the path on the opposite direction, ready to box us in, and neither caravan was carrying any cargo.

When the smuggler realized we'd found out his ambush, he threw a satchel into the fire that burnt me and several of his own men. All hell broke out afterwards.

They brought sheer numbers to the fight, and attempted to lift our mount, Bingus, up along the cliffside to claim him for their own (we learned later that Moleskin is evidentally highly prized in this area of the world). We suspect they may have intended to capture us as well, though the others certianly put up too much of a fight for them to do anything but brawl with us. Grem ended up saving me by using one of her rings to teleport me onto Bingus's back, and the entire party decided to flee not to long after. I'm not sure how many we managed to dispatch during that spell, but there were a good twenty men who sprung us in that ambush.

I wasn't entirely there for most of the chase thereafter, but Bingus must have taken a wrong turn somehow, because we found ourselves falling into the heart of the smuggler's camp. We fell into some sort of fighting ring, a good ten feet deep, and became trapped. Bingus was too tired to flee, and we were panicked from the ambush.

We didn't have much to show for it, but we ended up all attempting to bluff our way out of the camp. It was much like how animals puff themselves up to appear larger and more threatening than they are. We shrouded the arena in darkness, and Grem put on airs to frighten and beguile the camp residents, insisting she defeated all the men they'd sent after us and had eaten them.

This worked quite well on most of the smugglers, but their leader was not so impressed. The bird-man, a crow by the name of Corbin, was more amused with our antics than intimitated. He talked with us a while though, belittling his own men and urging us to work with him. While he was chatting with Grem, I realized that we'd actually been sent to deliver an invitation to this man long ago, though we hadn't expected to find him anywhere near the area.

Corbin was delighted to recive his invitation from the Oni, and showed particular interest in meeting up with the ghost we'd saved from Dagatar's building. Corbin, however, insisted that we entertain him before he agreed to go to the party. We explained we did not have a horse in this particular race, but regardless Corbin insisted. He sicc'ed two of his new recruits on us in the arena.

Thankfully, these two recruits were neither threats nor enemies. We recognized them as Jasper and Jimmy, two of the rust knights who we'd chatted with the second day Luisa, Noggin, and I had arrived in this place. Jimmy didn't take much convinicing to sit out of the fight, and while Jasper was eager for a rematch against Luisa, he did not stand a chance against the lady.

The crowd was pretty pleased by the fight though, and Corbin insisted on fighting Luisa as a result. We tried to talk him out of it, but he insisted, so we let Luisa beat the ever-loving shit out of the bird. I'm not quite sure what he was expecting, but Luisa knocked him out cold. 

The smuggler's camp was in high spirits following the fight (I got the impression they didn't care for their leader all too much), and Luisa took the man's feather cloak as a trophy. We walked out of there without any issues, and invited Jasper and Jimmy along (Jimmy joined up with us, Jasper was still injured and unconscious, but he should be fine in a while). While we figured the ravine may be safe for us after Luisa's matches, we left with a bitter taste in our mouths for the entire region, and wanted to get out of there before resting.

We've set up camp about a half-hour down the ravine, heading towards the plantation. Jimmy has been quiet and has largely just been tending to his older(?) brother. I don't think we have to worry about too much trouble from them.

I'm able to do some of Luisa's training tonight, just not well. I had a gun pointing to the head of the smuggler who dropped me, and wasn't able to protect my face in the blast of fire afterwards, but I can feel it healing already.
