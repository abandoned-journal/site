When we woke in the morning, we agreed to head towards the Lesser Decay and take our chances with whatever strange lake was over there. Our goal was to reach the capital city and see what we could find out about Grem's whereabouts.

Upon reaching the lake, we found it like the locals had claimed: the entire body of water had been replaced with something... odd. We took great caution not to touch the substance, but Noggin procured some samples and Luisa tested the liquid with a few objects. We found that it transported any organic matter placed into it, and moved it elsewhere.

This was honestly much less unnerving than I had originally thought, given how the place was described. We talked briefly about potentially using the liquid as a means of transportation, but figured it'd be safer to walk around. After all, we didn't really understand what the substance was, or why it was acting the way it did.

There was also a strange noise in the air, an animalistic sound deep and low, like some great beast breathing its final breaths. The air was stale and strange, filled with some substance that seemed neither harmful, nor beniegn. We covered our faces to be safe.

Noggin tested some ice magic while we were out examining the "water". He used a spell I had never seen before, which he called "Dragon's Breath". Luisa seemed mildly annoyed at the name, and later insisted that Noggin's height had changed. I'm not sure if I agree with her on that front, but I did watch the word "SOBER" appear on Noggin's forehead out of the blue, so I'm assuming something strange was at play with Noggin again.

As we continued to walk around the lake, we saw a man out in the distance, constantly running like a madman and teleporting in fiery blasts. We figured perhaps he had falled into the lake, and this is perhaps what it did to people. Before we flagged him down, we did get close enough to realize that he was the same race of vampires we had fought with the day prior. Regardless, we thought it best to hail him.

When we drew the man's attention, he became feral and charged towards us, threatening violence. We had a rotten deer carcass, and tossed it his way to try to dissuade any bloodlust. Thankfully, this placated the man, and restored his sanity enough to converse with us.

The man, Xibaqua, explained to us that we were trapped in the Lesser Decay with him. He had come here, as a member of the Trirose Syndicate, and was investigating why shipments had stopped passing through the area. However, he'd become trapped by the region merely by entering its borders, and now could not stray too far from the lake without it teleporting him back.

We put this to the test immediately, asking Luisa to throw her teleporting hand-axe to see if she could warp out of the land's borders. She was able to successfully warp for a moment, but after landing Luisa dissapeared and the axe fell to the ground. Luisa appeared back alongside us in a fire-y burst, and reported a strong confusion and could not recall the last several minutes prior to the teleport.

Seeing as we were now trapped in the area with Xibaqua, we took some time to tend to him and ask about his people. We first learned that the race we were calling vampires are actually the Tanggal, and did not feed solely on blood. The Tanggal primarily feed upon works of the soul: poetry, writing, music, any sort of craftsmanship it seems. It made sense to us after that why they had taken Grem, as she was the only one in the party who likely would have interested them. Because we had no other fresh food for the Tanggal, we offered him Luisa's journal entries she'd written up after Dagatar. I'm not sure what the contents of those pages were, but Xibaqua seemed both sated and highly critical of her work. They've agreed to be pen pals.

We also talked a bit about the Tanggal's presence in the region. The Tanggal were from the same time as the Oni, and can live for long periods of time, perhaps even surpassing elves. Xibaqua himself had been trapped in the wastes here for many years by the time we found him. The bifurcating trick the other Tanggal had shown us in combat is evidentally something that he (and presumably all?) the Tanggal can do, and he demonstrated how he can levitate using his upperbody. We asked a bit about the Trirose Syndicate as well, since I was curious why they were peddling drugs when they needed some degree of agency in their people to produce artistic works. Xibaqua admitted that side of the business is indeed money motivated.

Most importantly though, we discussed how to leave the Lesser Wastes, and Xibaqua pointed us in the direction of an old mine he believed to be relevant. He had avoided investigating the matter because the mine was filled with several ghost-like serpents, incorporeal and perhaps best dealt with by magic. We decided we'd risk an encounter with the creatures, and he accompanied us towards the mine.

Along the way, I found an interesting bit of oni technology: a gun. It's a small pistol with no chamber for bullets, two triggers, and a lot of interesting filigree. Xibaqua warned me against using it since it was probably some dangerous Oni mining tech but it looks like something I can use in a pinch.

I know from Identifying the object that it's an old mining laser, and can approximately figure out how safe it is to use and what range I can expect on it. I also know I fired it at least once, but...

Truth be told, I have no recollection of the fight that took place after I picked up that gun. The next thing I remembered was falling out of the sky, half-dead, and in a completely different spot than I had been. I remember walking a bit further, but everything goes fuzzy beyond that. It was a similar sensation to when I first arrived in this world, a similar amnesia to the one that forced me to write this journal. [A line here has been erased]

Anyways, I woke up feeling like I'd been treading water in an icy lake for hours. I was miserably cold, and confused, but nothing seemed an immediate threat anymore. Noggin and Xibaqua told us that they'd been waiting for Luisa and I to re-appear, so I'm guessing we somehow made contact with the lake water. It looked like there'd been fighting around, but I'm not sure what or how anything went down.

Nor did we have time to discuss things further, since Grem had arrived! Grem reappeared through some sort of portal, joining us in the Lesser Wastes, alongside a severed hand. We were ecstatic to reunite with Grem, but Xibaqua seemed rather distressed over the hand that had accompanied her, correctly identifying it as a Tanggal hand. Luisa collected the jewelry off of it, while Xibaqua was panicking. 

Grem later recounted that she had been abducted by the Tanggal over the quality of her performance. They had scouted her, and whisked her away before she could protest leaving us behind. She'd spent some time in the capital alongside other artists, and had become something of a hit with the organization. When offered an endorsement from the eldest Tanggal, Absolm, Grem had lied (?) that she had a son (?????) and was apprehensive over accepting the offer but was considering it. Her patron (??????????) intervened over the matter and did divine battle against Absolm, depositing Grem back with us in the meantime.

This did lead to some follow-up questions regarding Grem's patron, considering she was not a warlock when we first met a few weeks prior. Grem explained that she had been contacted by the Oni gods and formed a pact with them recently. I find that and Noggin's Nullity situation a bit concerning, but they do seem the preferential gods to have on our side here.

Thankfully, Noggin and Xibaqua were able to wrap up the situation at the Lesser Wastes after Grem's appearance. Noggin was able to either shut down or remove some bit of deep drilling machinery that was cutting into the planet, and the black liquid seeping out of the earth became transparent and... more like just plain water. Xibaqua was the first to test out whether we were free, ran towards the horizon, and didn't return. 

Noggin told us there was a strange, flesh-like substance in the earth where the drill was cutting into it, so I made the trek down to see it better. Sure enough, the planet (or at least this region of it), appears to be alive, with a hard carapace beneath the dirt and flesh beneath. I attempted to heal the flesh and carapace, similar to how I'd done with the spider back in the underground city, and it seemed to have a small effect. Luisa dragged me out of there though before I could spend much time in the depths.

With Grem reunited with the group, we decided it best to avoid the capital, seeing as we likely cut off the hand of the head Tanggal. It'll be a difficult journey with the added attention, but I'm more confident about sneaking around the city than charging into it.

I'm also glad we had a chance to speak with Xibaqua and learn about the Tanggal. They seem dangerous, as does the Trirose Syndicate, but I'm still glad we helped Xibaqua out of his situation. I hope a similar situation isn't occurring over at the Greater Decay, but if it is, at least Xibaqua is now aware of how to resolve the situation and can spread word.

I'm also, oddly enough, grateful to have a firearm. I used to shoot a bit as a kid, but never used one in service. Jakab always made me carry his derringer into the field if we were out after a battle, but they always cleaned things up before we arrived. Apart from Sellve, there was never a civilian who survived the onslaught, let alone an enemy. I was never in danger.

I don't think he knew that I knew, but Jakab always kept that derringer on him, even after he'd make a show about disarming his main pistol in front of the higher-ups. Knives too, he kept one on his belt, on in the upper pocket in front of his heart, and one in each boot. They were thin, and rectangular, like he didn't want them to be recognized as knives, but what else would they be?

The knife I got from Riawat, I do like it. This new derringer is good as well. But, Jakab was better with a gun than I am, and better with a knife, and they still gutted him easily enough. He didn't have magic though, and I've figured that much out. The workouts too... they'll add up to something, I'm sure. Knife, gun, magic, muscle... I'm shit at them all but they're all there. If I just train harder, get better with them all... I can figure out where to go from here. I'll figure it out.

Hell, my chest is black from frostbite. I can probably heal it, but what if I need the magic for later? Better to sleep it off?
